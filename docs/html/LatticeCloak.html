LatticeCloaktyped placeholders and truthful generalization lattices for closed-box LLM inference

research report · 2026-07-02
Abstract

LatticeCloak anonymizes prompts for closed-box LLM inference: typed placeholders replace direct identifiers, truthful generalizations from per-span lattices replace quasi-identifiers, and a client-side substitution record lets rules invert the remote answer. Its per-token noise predecessor tied identity removal, premise preservation, and recovery to one scalar budget; LatticeCloak separates them. Direct-identifier detection reached 0.998 observed recall on the Text Anonymization Benchmark, and 97.8% of detected SynthPAI spans routed to zero-cost sources, leaving a 36-entity teacher residue. The per-span threshold τ shifted lattice levels (26→34 accepted at most-specific), yet aggregate surface probes stayed flat (lexical overlap 0.989, utility 0.971–0.972) and only a candidate-sensitive masked-LM attack responded, so pricing the knob requires an adversarial attacker: that evaluation, and the privacy-utility Pareto, are the explicitly pending next stage; round-trip evidence is smoke-scale.

§01

Introduction

problem · threat/setup · headline result

LatticeCloak is the successor to the abandoned InferDPT/RANTEXT line: instead of per-token ε-LDP swaps, it replaces direct identifiers with typed placeholders and quasi-identifiers with truthful generalizations drawn from a per-span lattice, keeping a client-side substitution record R that a rule-based extractor inverts on the way back. On the TAB benchmark the detection stack reaches 0.998 observed direct-identifier recall (the gate for the placeholder path), 97.5–97.8% of detected spans on SynthPAI route to zero-cost sources (rules, GeoNames, WordNet), and the full round trip runs end to end, with constructed prompts showing facts restored wherever the remote answer cites a recoverable substituted span.

The setting is a privacy layer for closed-box inference: a local client rewrites a prompt doc_orig into an anonymized doc_p, sends it to an untrusted remote LLM, receives out_p, and locally maps it back to out_final. The predecessor report (InferDPT & RANTEXT) established why the per-token mechanism fails: one scalar ε jointly governs identity removal, premise preservation, and extraction signal, and the end-to-end utility of that pipeline was produced by the extractor seeing the raw document, not by the perturbation. LatticeCloak decomposes the knob: placeholders carry the identifier load exactly and reversibly, lattices carry the premise load truthfully at an adjustable coarseness, and a masked-token probe (τ) sets the operating point per span rather than globally.

Lineage. The lattice+τ mechanism composes three prior lines. The lattice itself — ordered candidates, most-specific first — is a k-anonymity value-generalization hierarchy (Sweeney 2002, DOI 10.1142/S0218488502001648), realized per span type via GeoNames chains, WordNet hypernyms, rule buckets, and teacher-written lattices. Choosing a level as a truthful generalization against inference risk — not deletion or falsification — follows INTACT (Pilán et al. 2024, arXiv 2412.12928) and self-disclosure abstraction (Dou et al. 2024, arXiv 2311.09538). τ itself is a masked-token-inference guess-back probe (roberta-base, DP-MLM/Meisenbacher family): mask the candidate, score whether an MLM recovers the original, accept the first level with risk < τ. LatticeCloak's own part is the combination — a k-anonymity lattice driven by an MTI attacker-proxy thresholded per span, replacing global k / global ε with a per-span, inference-gated choice (whose privacy value the pending frontier-attacker Pareto must still settle).

This page documents the prototype design and the measurements taken so far: the detection gate on TAB, span routing and teacher-cascade economics on SynthPAI, and a smoke-scale round trip with rule-only extraction. The adversarial evaluation (frontier-LLM attribute inference at matched realized privacy against the InferDPT baseline) is the next stage and is not claimed here.

§03

Method

design choices · roles · pipeline stages

Design choices. Three commitments shape the system. First, no autoregressive LLM runs in the deployed local path: the substitutor decomposes into detection, lookup, ranking, and inversion, each served by encoders or dictionaries (the whole stack is under 1B parameters and batches on an iGPU). Second, substitution is deterministic and auditable: replacements come from rule buckets, geographic and lexical hierarchies, or a cached teacher lattice, never from proximity sampling in an embedding space, which the predecessor page showed either preserves the referent or destroys the premise. Third, every failure is designed to fall toward abstention: an unresolvable span becomes a coarse type label (utility cost) rather than a leak (privacy cost).

Detection. GLiNER takes TAB's eight entity categories as free-text zero-shot labels, the only surveyed detector covering DEM, QUANTITY and CODE at all; Presidio contributes pattern recognizers (reference codes, money including spelled-out amounts, contact data). Spans merge widest-wins; pure-symbol spans and bare pronouns are dropped; lowercase "person" hits (occupations, roles) are retyped as demographic attributes so they generalize instead of becoming placeholders. Alias chains give every name variant one chain id, so "Sarah Johnson" and "Sarah" share one placeholder.

Lattice building. Dates and quantities bucket by rule ("March 3, 2021" → "March 2021" → "the early 2020s"; exact values never survive into any level). Places walk the GeoNames hierarchy; common nouns walk WordNet hypernyms. Only the residue, named entities no offline source covers, reaches the teacher cascade: E4B writes three candidate levels from the mention in context; every level must pass the NLI gate (with degenerate-substitution and self-reference rejections); failures regenerate once through Qwen3.6 with a chain-of-thought prompt; double failures floor to the type label. Results cache to disk keyed by entity and sense context, so runtime substitution is a dictionary lookup.

The cascade itself is the one component with internal moving parts:

FIG · M2 — teacher cascade with the NLI truthfulness gate (all local, offline, cached) measured on 36 entities: 31 settled at E4B · 4 escalated · 1 floored

Substitution. Direct identifiers become numbered typed placeholders per chain (<PERSON_1>, <CODE_2>). Each quasi identifier walks its lattice most specific first and accepts the first level whose MTI guess-back risk is below τ; candidates carrying the original's numbers or proper names are filtered before the walk. The output is doc_p plus R.

How the τ-walk chooses, and what it cannot see. Every choice is a fixed local heuristic. When the substitutor hits a quasi-identifier like "Oslo" it has a ladder of candidate replacements — the lattice, most specific first:

levelreplacement
0Oslo (original, most specific)
1a Norwegian city
2a Scandinavian capital
3a European city (coarsest)

The walk starts at level 0, runs the cheap local MTI probe ("if I mask this span, does an MLM guess 'Oslo' back?"), and takes the first level whose guess-back risk drops below the threshold τ. This decision is made per span, in isolation, using only the probe — it never sees what happens downstream: whether the remote LLM's answer survived the coarsening, whether the extractor could still map the output back, or whether the combination of choices across spans re-identifies the person anyway. That myopia is the operating assumption this prototype tests and the limitation the co-design next stage is meant to address.

Extraction. Rung A inverts R without any model: placeholders swap back exactly; generalization mentions in out_p are located by word-boundary exact match, then by fuzzy alignment snapped to word boundaries, and replaced with the original surface. A learned rung B (flan-t5-base LoRA over synthesized inversions) is specified but deliberately unbuilt until the measured rung A residue justifies it; the gap between the two is the prototype's central hypothesis test.

Results

by pipeline stage

§5.1 Detection gate on TAB

All 127 TAB test documents, gold = union of all annotators' DIRECT/QUASI mentions (the strictest recall target), any-character-overlap matching, 44 s wall time. Aggregate: DIRECT any-recall 0.998 (typed 0.980, n=407), entity-level DIRECT recall 0.997, QUASI any-recall 0.857 (typed 0.783, n=6,524), precision proxy 0.716. The gate rule (DIRECT any-recall ≥ 0.95) passes, so the placeholder path does not need gold-span fallback.

Readviolet = DIRECT, green = QUASI. Direct identifiers are near-ceiling everywhere; the quasi weaknesses concentrate in DEM, MISC and QUANTITY, the categories needing document-level reasoning rather than span matching.

Conclusion. The detection gate passes (0.998 any-overlap, 0.997 entity-level, both observed on this test set). The measured weak categories (QUASI DEM 0.56, MISC 0.21, QUANTITY 0.25 any-recall) are the fuzzy "demographic attribute / other identifying event" spans; on the round-trip corpus their impact is priced by the attacker, not hidden by the detector. One smoke-run fix mattered: every initially missed DIRECT mention was an ECtHR application number, eliminated by a single numeric-reference pattern rather than threshold tuning.

§5.2 Span routing and teacher economics on SynthPAI

Routing of detected spans across lattice sources on the full 300-profile corpus (2,438 spans, post noise-cleanup: pure-symbol spans, bare pronouns and anchor-free temporals excluded). The teacher cascade serves 2.2% of spans (54 mentions, 36 unique entities); everything else is rules, GeoNames, WordNet, or placeholders. Full-corpus detection takes 49 s; the cascade settled 31 of 36 entities at E4B, escalated 4 to Qwen3.6, floored 1. The 60-document working slice used elsewhere on this page routes the same way (teacher share 2.5%, same rank order of sources), so the slice is representative of the corpus for routing purposes.

Conclusion. The zero-call bootstrap thesis holds at corpus scale: 97.8% of detected spans never touch a generative model, the full-corpus lattice cache builds in under two minutes on-box, and E4B handled most of the pilot's lattice writing (4/36 escalations, 1 floor). Scaling the substitutor is not a cost concern; the metered cost lives entirely in the evaluation round trips.

§5.3 The τ knob: leakage and utility probes across operating points

LatticeCloak has no privacy budget ε; its operating-point knob is τ, the guess-back threshold. Lowering τ demands lower contextual inferability before a lattice level is accepted, pushing quasi spans toward coarser levels and type-label floors; raising it admits more specific levels. Unlike ε it acts per span, not globally. To read LatticeCloak on the same instruments as the InferDPT report, the same probe families were computed over a τ sweep {0.005, 0.02, 0.1, 0.5} on the smoke slice (8 docs, 32 task answers per point). Leakage: overlap (the fraction of doc_orig's content words surviving verbatim into doc_p), PII leak (fuzzy survival of probe-detected PII spans), and MLM guess-back, the span analog of their inversion@10: the attacker reads doc_p with the replacement visible and a masked-LM guesses the original slot (top-50 hit rate; candidate-sensitive, so specific levels help the attacker). Utility: utility_control (SimCSE of out_final against the no-privacy control answer), utility_p_control (same for the raw out_p), coherence(gen_p) (SimCSE of out_p against its own prompt doc_p), and coherence(doc_p) (SimCSE of doc_p against doc_orig). Corpora and tasks differ between the two reports, so scales are comparable per instrument, not across pages. FIG 03 toggles across three eval regimes: the extend-prefix probes just described (SynthPAI), and the τ reruns on the two task-oriented corpora (clinical dialogue→note, email→reply), where utility is scored as ROUGE-L and BERTScore of out_final against the gold output (out_ctrl = no-privacy reference) and the mechanism panel is the share of generalized spans left at the most-specific level vs floored to the type label.

Read

Conclusion. τ demonstrably moves the substitution operating point (mechanism panel), but the aggregate surface instruments barely register it: overlap is 0.989 everywhere (span-targeted substitution leaves the document's lexical surface intact by design, unlike per-token perturbation, for which overlap is the natural instrument), coherence(doc_p) is 0.994 everywhere (the perturbed document stays semantically faithful), utility_control is 0.971–0.972 with out_final = out_p (inversion unexercised on these tasks), and PII leak is 0.842 at every τ. Inspection shows that survival number is dominated by the probe's own detector counting deliberately kept generic temporals ("daily", "Sundays") and misdetections ("Nah", "USB"), though it also catches one genuine miss class: an education phrase ("Sci Master's") survives, consistent with the DEM weakness measured on TAB. The one instrument that responds is the candidate-sensitive one: MLM guess-back rises from 0.18 to 0.21 at the loosest τ, directionally as designed, though within noise for 36 spans. The methodological consequence is the predecessor taxonomy's F4 lesson landing on this method too: aggregate surface probes cannot price LatticeCloak's knob; attack-shaped instruments respond, and the full adversarial evaluation is what the next stage runs.

The task-oriented reruns (FIG 03, clinical and email tabs; 16 docs each) sharpen the same point on reference-scored utility. On clinical the knob is inert: nearly all 586 generalized spans already sit at the most-specific level even at τ = 0.005 (their contextual guess-back is already low), so ROUGE-L (~0.25) and BERTScore (~0.85) are flat. On email the knob is live — τ visibly repositions the lattice (share at most-specific 0.82→0.98, floors 0.74→0.58 as τ loosens) — yet task utility still moves only within noise (ROUGE-L 0.096→0.098). The leakage panel confirms the pattern: overlap and PII survival are flat on both corpora, and the one instrument that responds is again the candidate-sensitive MLM guess-back, which rises 0.31→0.35 on email (where the lattice moves) while staying flat at 0.14 on clinical (where it does not) — the same attack-shaped signal the extend-prefix regime showed. Both cases make the methodological verdict concrete: whether or not τ moves the mechanism, it is not legible in the end-to-end task metric, so the operating point has to be priced against an adversary. (Utility is low in absolute terms on both — clinical notes and email replies are one-reference generation tasks — which is why the privatized/control gap, not the level, is the quantity the Pareto will report.)

§06

Findings

claims · grounds · scope

The results license architectural and component-level fixes to specific InferDPT/RANTEXT failure modes; they do not yet license a privacy–utility win over InferDPT (Finding 8 is the gate). Verdicts below were adjudicated by an external model against all measured results (trace in .aris/traces/result-to-claim/).

1 · Direct-identifier removal is near-ceiling and exactly reversible on TAB — an improvement over ε-noise (Partial). Any-overlap DIRECT recall 0.998, entity-level 0.997, typed 0.980 (n=407, 127 docs); detected direct spans become typed placeholders that swap back exactly, where token-level ε both destroys the surface and cannot invert. The improvement is scoped to detection + reversibility, not privacy: any-overlap matching is lenient, the numeric-reference regex that cleared the miss class was added after inspecting five docs of the same test set (not a frozen hold-out), and reversibility is smoke-scale (Finding 5). Grounds: §5.1, §5.4.

2 · Quasi-identifier detection is weak on DEM, MISC, QUANTITY and CODE (Supported, negative). QUASI any-recall DEM 0.56, MISC 0.21, QUANTITY 0.25, CODE 0.76 against 0.86 overall (n=6,524). These open-class, contextual spans are recognized by inference, not matching; misses pass into doc_p unprotected. The detector number alone cannot price this — only the (pending) attacker can. Grounds: §5.1.

3 · Substitution is cheap and mostly local — no autoregressive model in the hot path (Yes, scoped). 97.8% of 2,438 detected spans route to zero-cost sources (rules, GeoNames, WordNet, placeholders); the generative teacher covers 36 unique entities across 300 profiles, cached in under two minutes on-box. Scope: measured corpora only — this is a cost/routing result, not a substitution-quality audit, and needs multi-domain replication. Grounds: §5.2.

4 · A 4B-class local teacher wrote most lattices when gated by a verification cascade (Partial). 31/36 entities settled at E4B, 4 escalated to Qwen3.6, 1 floored; the NLI gate plus degeneracy and self-reference rejections caught the observed failure classes. Adequacy in general is unestablished: n=36, one corpus, no blind accuracy of accepted candidates, no gate false-accept rate. Grounds: §5.2, §3.

5 · The raw-document extractor leak is closed by design; rule-only inversion recovers cited facts for exact/near-exact restatement only (Partial). Recovery reads the client-side record R, never the raw document — structurally removing InferDPT's E1 leak (its end-to-end utility came from the extractor seeing doc_orig). After the lowercase-name routing fix, direct-identifier inversion now fires (clinical ph_swapped 9, gen_exact 7; email ph_swapped 4), and constructed prompts show facts restored ("a financial institution"→Goldman Sachs, "a city in Illinois"→Chicago). But rule-only inversion misses paraphrase, reformatting, narrowing and deliberately-suppressed precision (three residue classes, §5.4), and the SynthPAI smoke left inversion unexercised (128/128 substituted spans absent from answers). Evidence is smoke-scale (8–16 docs) plus four constructed prompts. Grounds: §5.4.

6 · τ separates the operating point per span, but is not a legible aggregate or task knob (Partial). Across the three-regime sweep (FIG 03): on clinical the lattice is pinned at most-specific even at τ=0.005 (583→586 of 586, τ inert); on email τ genuinely repositions it (most-specific 115→137, floors 103→81); on extend-prefix 26→34. Yet every aggregate/task instrument stays flat — overlap, PII survival, ROUGE-L/BERTScore and SimCSE do not move — and the only responsive one is the candidate-sensitive MLM guess-back (email 0.31→0.35, extend 0.19→0.21, clinical flat 0.14). So the knob is observable, not yet shown to be a controllable privacy–utility dial. Grounds: §5.3.

7 · Task utility is not yet a win, and inversion is often inert (Partial, negative). On the task corpora out_final ≈ out_p: clinical ROUGE-L 0.248–0.250 vs no-privacy control 0.218 (a small, one-reference gain), email 0.096–0.098 below control 0.124. These are one-reference generation metrics, so the privatized–control gap, not the level, is what a Pareto would report; on current tasks the gap is small or negative. Grounds: §5.3, §5.4.

8 · No privacy–utility improvement over InferDPT is demonstrated (Not supported — the gate). There is no head-to-head at matched realized privacy, no frontier-LLM re-identification/attribute-inference attacker on doc_p or out_final, and the MLM guess-back is a weak candidate-sensitive proxy, not an adversary. Every "better than InferDPT" statement in this report is therefore architectural/component-level and must be gated by this until the Pareto stage runs. Grounds: §5.3, §08.

§07

Analysis

why each finding holds — the mechanism behind the numbers

Why direct recall is near ceiling while quasi lags. Direct identifiers are surface-regular (names, codes) and sit squarely in the detector's pattern and NER competence; the one systematic miss class (application numbers) was a regex away. DEM/MISC/QUANTITY spans are open-class and contextual ("a mother of three", "the incident that spring"): recognizing them is inference, not matching, which is the known boundary of span-level detection and the reason the evaluation design measures privacy as attacker outcome rather than detector recall. This asymmetry is why the honest improvement over ε-noise is confined to the direct-identifier path: there LatticeCloak both detects near-ceiling and inverts exactly, whereas the quasi path inherits detection's contextual ceiling.

Why the teacher residue is so small. SynthPAI's quasi-identifiers are dominated by occupations, education, locations, ages and incomes: common nouns, places and values, which WordNet, GeoNames and rule buckets already cover. Only branded or culturally specific entities (Netflix, IKEA, konbini) fall through, and their number grows sublinearly with corpus size because entities repeat — which is what makes the "no autoregressive model in the hot path" property hold at corpus scale.

Why the cascade's checks are load-bearing. Three observed failure classes never reached the cache: fluent wrong-sense hypernyms for rare foreign terms, vacuous entailments from degenerate substitutions in fragmentary contexts, and candidates containing the entity itself. Each was caught by a targeted rejection (case-insensitive substitution with fail-closed matching, repeated-word degeneracy check, self-reference filter) in front of the entailment score, not by the score alone. One probe property matters for interpretation: masking hides the candidate, so MTI risk measures whether the context gives the original away, and a span inferable at every lattice level correctly lands on the most general form with its risk recorded in R.

Why τ is inert on clinical but live on email. The walk accepts a level once its masked guess-back risk falls below τ; whether that happens at the most-specific level depends on how inferable the span is from context. Clinical spans (ages, conditions, med names in a scaffolded note dialogue) have low contextual guess-back already, so even the strictest τ=0.005 accepts level 0 — the lattice is pinned and the knob does nothing. Email spans (dates, organizations in short threads) are more contextually inferable, so tightening τ pushes them to coarser levels — the lattice moves. This also explains the leakage panel: only the candidate-sensitive MLM guess-back rises with τ on email, because more specific levels hand the attacker more signal; overlap and PII survival, which read the untouched surrounding text, cannot see a handful of re-leveled spans on either corpus.

Why the aggregate/task metrics can't price τ. The sweep re-chooses levels for tens to hundreds of spans inside documents whose remaining content is untouched, so document-level overlap and reference-scored ROUGE-L/BERTScore are dominated by everything the substitution left alone. Per-token ε perturbation, which rewrites every token, is the regime those probes were built for. The operating point of a span-targeted method has to be priced where its effect lives — in what an adversary can infer — which is exactly why the pending frontier-attacker Pareto, not these instruments, is the load-bearing evaluation.

Why closing the extractor leak matters but buys less than it did for InferDPT. InferDPT's measured end-to-end utility came from its extractor reading doc_orig; LatticeCloak moves recovery entirely client-side (invert R), so the remote model and the extractor never see the raw document — the leak is gone by construction. The catch is that this only helps when the answer actually restates a substituted span: summaries and short QA compress and paraphrase, dropping exactly the specifics, so on those tasks out_final ≈ out_p and inversion is inert. Extraction value therefore concentrates in restatement-heavy tasks (notes, replies), where the rung A residue classes (rephrase misses, grammar seams, suppressed precision) become the binding constraint and a learned rung B either earns its keep or does not.

what it buys · implications · limitations · next

The gate (read this first). Nothing here is a privacy–utility improvement over InferDPT. There is no head-to-head at matched realized privacy, no frontier-LLM attacker on doc_p or out_final, and the one responsive in-house probe (MLM guess-back) is a weak candidate-sensitive proxy, not an adversary. What the results license are architectural and component-level fixes to specific InferDPT/RANTEXT failure modes — not a better frontier.

What it buys over InferDPT (scoped). The predecessor located InferDPT's failure in one scalar ε doing three jobs, in RANTEXT degenerating on a faithful mechanism, and in its utility coming from an extractor that read the raw document. LatticeCloak addresses each at the mechanism level: identity removal is a typed, exactly reversible placeholder rather than a destructive random swap (near-ceiling direct detection on TAB); premises are coarsened by truthful generalizations that never feed the remote model a falsehood; substitution is mostly local dictionaries/rules (97.8% zero-call); and recovery reads a client-side record R, so the extractor never sees doc_orig — closing the E1 leak by construction. The remote model reasoning on coarsened premises, including declining to invent removed facts, is a property token-level noise could not deliver. Each of these is a component result at the measured scope, not an end-to-end win.

Where it underperforms or is unproven. (1) The central gap is the missing matched-privacy comparison above. (2) Quasi-identifier detection is weak on DEM/MISC/QUANTITY/CODE, and those misses pass into doc_p unprotected; implicit disclosures ("my 9-to-5 at the hospital") never enter any span denominator at all. (3) τ is not a legible operating dial: it is inert on clinical (spans pinned at most-specific) and, even where it moves the lattice (email), leaves every aggregate/task metric flat. (4) Utility is not a win — out_final ≈ out_p on most tasks, email sits below the no-privacy control, and clinical's gain is small under a one-reference metric. (5) Round-trip evidence is smoke-scale (8–16 docs + four constructed prompts) and rule-only inversion is brittle to paraphrase, reformatting and suppressed precision; no learned rung B exists. (6) The evaluation itself carries leakage risks: lenient any-overlap matching, a regex tuned after inspecting the test set, a precision proxy, and no frozen hold-out. (7) Residual substitution-quality defects — detection misfires (Anthrax/Bunche→PERSON), coref over-merge (Doctor Kumar), and the GeoNames sense prior (Cambridge UK vs MA) — are accepted rule-based ceilings deferred to the learned substitutor.

Next. The Pareto stage that turns the scoped component claims into (or refutes as) an end-to-end result: τ sweep against the InferDPT ε sweep on identical documents and tasks, privacy scored by held-out frontier attackers on doc_p and out_final, utility scored against references and gold answers; then rung B trained on synthesized inversions to price the measured rung A residue. Kill criteria are pre-registered: if LatticeCloak does not beat InferDPT at matched realized privacy, that is the finding; if rung B does not beat rules there, R plus rules suffice.
